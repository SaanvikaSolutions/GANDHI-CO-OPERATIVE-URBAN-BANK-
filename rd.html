Types of Recurring Deposits

Regular Recurring Deposit

  • Recurring deposit amount at regular intervals.
  • Steady interest rates throughout the tenure.
  • Fixed tenure chosen by the investor.

Flexible Recurring Deposit

  • Allows variation in deposit amounts within limits.
  • Offers flexibility to adjust the deposit tenure.
  • Interest rates remain fixed despite changes in deposits or tenure.

Senior Citizen Recurring Deposit

  • Provides higher interest rates for elderly investors.
  • Offers special benefits like priority services and fee waivers.
  • Requires meeting a minimum age eligibility criterion, typically above 55 or 60 years.

Premature Withdrawal

Premature withdrawal from an RD is typically allowed, but it may incur penalties or forfeiture of a portion of the interest earned, depending on the bank's terms and conditions.

Flexible Tenure

Ghandhi Cooperative Bank's offer flexibility in the tenure of RDs, allowing investors to choose durations according to their financial goals and preferences.

Interest Rates

Banks offer fixed interest rates on RDs, usually higher than savings accounts but lower than fixed deposits (FDs). These rates may vary based on the duration of the RD and prevailing market conditions.

No Market Risk

Unlike investment options like stocks or mutual funds, RDs do not expose investors to market risks since they offer fixed returns.

Premature Withdrawal

Premature withdrawal from an RD is typically allowed, but it may incur penalties or forfeiture of a portion of the interest earned, depending on the bank's terms and conditions. 

Auto-Renewal

At the end of the RD tenure, some banks may offer the option to automatically renew the RD for another term, ensuring continued savings and investment.
