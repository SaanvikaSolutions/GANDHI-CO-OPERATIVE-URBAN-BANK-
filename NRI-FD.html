Features

Repatriation

NRI Fixed Deposits allow Non-Resident Indians to repatriate both the principal amount and the interest earned to their country of residence freely. This feature provides flexibility in managing funds across borders.

Tax Implications

The taxation of NRI Fixed Deposits varies depending on the tax laws of the country where the deposit is held and the NRI's home country. Interest earned may be subject to taxation, and tax implications should be considered before investing.

Currency Options

NRI FDs are available in major foreign currencies like USD, GBP, EUR, AUD, CAD, etc. NRIs can choose the currency of their FD based on their preferences and investment goals.

Joint Holding

Some banks allow NRI FDs to be held jointly with another NRI or resident Indian. Joint holding provides flexibility in managing funds and facilitates estate planning.

Loan against FD

Certain banks offer loans against NRI FDs, allowing depositors to access funds without breaking the deposit prematurely. The loan amount is usually a percentage of the FD's value. 

Auto-Renewal

Upon maturity, NRI FDs may be automatically renewed for another term unless specified otherwise by the depositor. This ensures continuous investment and prevents funds from becoming idle
